<p><p>The automobile oxygen sensor market features various types designed to optimize engine performance and emissions control. Unheated thimble oxygen sensors, often used in older models, rely on exhaust gas temperature for measurement. Heated thimble sensors enhance responsiveness by heating up quickly. Planar oxygen sensors provide faster response times and lower production costs. Wide-band sensors offer precise air-fuel ratio measurements, making them ideal for advanced engine management systems. Titania sensors utilize titanium oxide to detect oxygen levels, contributing to efficient fuel combustion.</p></p>
