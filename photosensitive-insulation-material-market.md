<p><p>The photosensitive insulation material market is classified into two primary types: organic and inorganic materials. Organic photosensitive insulations are typically derived from carbon-based compounds, offering flexibility and ease of processing. They are often used in applications like flexible circuits and packaging. In contrast, inorganic materials are composed of non-carbon elements, providing greater thermal stability and durability. These materials are commonly employed in high-performance applications, such as aerospace and electronics, where resistance to environmental factors is crucial.</p></p>
